# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a respectful, inclusive, and collaborative environment
for everyone involved in the Charleston Design System project.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in language and actions
- Giving constructive and professional feedback
- Collaborating openly and transparently
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or offensive comments
- Personal attacks or hostile behavior
- Publishing private or confidential information without permission
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate action in response to unacceptable conduct
- Ensuring the project remains a safe and productive environment

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive or unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers.
All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- Git repositories
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Issues and discussions
- Any other communication channels related to the project

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by industry best practices and adapted for internal
and private project use.
